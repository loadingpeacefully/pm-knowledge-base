# Harvard Courses in Nano Skills

## Overview

Harvard Courses are a premium tier within the Nano Skills catalog — branded partnerships with Harvard-certified content. These courses require more diamonds than standard Nano Skills and are positioned as high-prestige offerings for students.

## Problem Statement

The Nano Skills catalog lacked differentiation in perceived value. Students with high diamond balances had no aspirational premium-tier product to spend on. Harvard-branded courses address the prestige gap and justify higher diamond prices.

## Goals & Success Metrics

- Drive at least 10% of Nano Skills diamond consumption through Harvard courses
- Increase high-balance student (>100 diamonds) activation rate
- Improve parent perception of value (NPS lift)
- Achieve 25% completion rate for enrolled Harvard courses

## User Stories / Jobs-to-Be-Done

- As a high-achieving student, I want access to Harvard-level content to learn beyond my grade
- As a parent, I want to see internationally recognized course names in my child's portfolio
- As a student, I want to show my Harvard course certificate to others
- As a student, I want to know what skills the Harvard course will teach me before enrolling

## Feature Scope

**In Scope:**
- Harvard course cards in Nano Skills catalog (distinct visual treatment)
- Higher diamond price prominently shown
- Certificate with Harvard branding on completion
- Harvard courses tray on Nano Skills homepage (separate from self-paced courses)
- Course detail page with curriculum and Harvard brand explanation

**Out of Scope:**
- Direct Harvard.edu integration or official Harvard credit
- Harvard courses for Financial Literacy (Coding only in V1)

## Key Design Decisions

- Harvard courses are NOT shown first to students with low diamond balances (< 15 diamonds) — Add Diamond banner takes priority
- Harvard course placement in tray is below Self-Paced and Language-Sensitive trays
- Completion certificate uses co-branded design (BrightCHAMPS + Harvard seal)

## Open Questions / Risks

- What is the source of "Harvard certification"? Need legal clarity on branding rights
- Risk: High price point may reduce trial enrollment
- Dependency: Certificate service must support co-branded template
