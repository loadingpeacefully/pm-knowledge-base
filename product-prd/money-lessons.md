# Money Lessons

## Overview

Curriculum and lesson design documentation for "Money" as a topic area within BrightCHAMPS' Financial Literacy (FinLit/AI) course. Covers scope, learning objectives, template usage, and connection to the gamified Adhyayan platform.

## Course Context: Financial Literacy (AI)

### About the FinLit Course
- One of BrightCHAMPS' flagship courses alongside Coding and Robotics
- Designed for students Grades 5–8 (primarily MEA region, then expanded globally)
- AI-styled presentation: animated character "Greenline" acts as an AI mentor/guide
- Replaced static Google Slides-based delivery with the interactive Adhyayan platform

### Why Money Is a Core Topic
- Foundational financial concept — universal across all demographics
- High parent relevance: parents want children to understand budgeting, saving, and value
- Strong "wow factor" during demo classes — captures parent attention immediately

## Money Lessons Scope

### Learning Objectives by Sub-Topic

**1. What Is Money?**
- Understand the purpose and history of money (barter → currency → digital)
- Recognize different forms of money (coins, notes, credit cards, digital payments)

**2. Earning Money**
- Concept of income: wages, salaries, allowances, business income
- Understanding "value exchange" — you provide value, you receive money

**3. Spending and Budgeting**
- Needs vs. wants distinction
- Simple budgeting: income − expenses = savings
- Making purchase decisions based on priority and available budget

**4. Saving and Goals**
- Short-term vs. long-term savings goals
- Concept of a savings target: set goal → track progress → achieve
- Introduction to "paying yourself first"

**5. Understanding Prices and Value**
- Comparison shopping: getting more for less
- Discounts, percentages, and calculating savings
- "Is this a good deal?" decision framework

## Gamified Template Usage

| Lesson | Template | Mechanic |
|--------|----------|----------|
| What Is Money? | Reorder | Arrange historical stages of money evolution |
| Earning Money | Bucketing | Categorize income types (earned vs. unearned) |
| Budgeting | Input / Fill Blanks | Calculate remaining savings from given income/expenses |
| Saving Goals | Card Flip | Reveal savings tips on card backs |
| Understanding Prices | Drag & Drop | Match items to correct price categories |

## Connection to "Shopping DNA" Activity

The "Shopping DNA" home activity (awarded 4.47/5 rating, 80%+ organic adoption) is a real-world extension of the Money lessons:
- Students complete a "Shopping DNA" quiz with parents to discover their spending personality
- Results shared in class for discussion
- Bridges classroom learning with home-context application

## Assessment Design

### Quiz Galaxy Integration
- Post-class Quiz Galaxy assessment covers Money lesson concepts
- 10 MCQ questions (mix of Easy/Medium)
- Revision flashcards available before quiz to reinforce key concepts
- Mastery threshold: 75% to "conquer" the planet (unlock next planet)

### Performance Data
- Quiz completion rate for Money lessons: tracked in Adhyayan platform
- Average score target: ≥80% for all students

## Teacher Delivery Notes

- Money topics resonate strongly with parents — encourage student to share examples from their own family (pocket money, grocery shopping)
- Use local currency examples for regional relevance (USD in US, INR in India, AED in UAE)
- "Shopping DNA" activity should be introduced at the end of the lesson with a clear parent action step
